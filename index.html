Circularity Midterm / Track & Trace

How we identified materials and translated them into the truss proposal

This board focuses only on the material identification step of the pavilion project. Starting from the ETH inventory spreadsheet, we selected reused elements whose dimensions and geometry could realistically support a lightweight, demountable truss structure, then tested that translation in the IFC-based proposal views.

Project frame

  • Temporary circular pavilion for ETH Zurich
  • Reusable materials only
  • Fully disassemblable and reversibly connected
  • Transportable by students in parts
  • Approximate footprint limit: 7.5 m x 7.5 m, max height: 3 m

Physical material logic

The selected inventory items were grouped by material behavior: timber members for supports, truss pieces for long spans, and planar wood elements for roof or floor surfaces. This panel stands in for the material photograph stage.

timber beams truss elements selected materials only dimensions from excel
03

Design translation

The proposed pavilion geometry uses the long recycled truss members as the defining side structure, with timber supports and reusable panel layers completing a lightweight enclosure. The IFC views were then checked to confirm proportion, span direction, and disassembly logic.

Primary side frames

2 of the 17 large Huber trusses are proposed for the two long side frames because their 719 cm length best matches the pavilion span.

Vertical supports

3 of the 5 unpainted timber beams are proposed as posts because they are simple to handle and compatible with reversible joints.

Secondary truss logic

1 of the 2 medium Huber trusses is proposed where the geometry reduces and a shorter bracing element is useful.

Assembly strategy

Pieces remain legible and separable so they can be transported to site, bolted together, and recovered after use.

Why this matters for circularity

Proposal logic
We are not starting with an ideal form and then buying new material to match it. Instead, the available reused ETH components define the span, support rhythm, and assembly strategy of the pavilion from the beginning.
Reuse first The proposal is based on stocked components rather than virgin material specifications.
Disassemblable Member-based construction supports reversible connections and future recovery.
Transportable Separate pieces can be carried and reassembled near the final site.
Traceable The route from spreadsheet entry to spatial role remains visible in the presentation.

This is a midterm proposal board, so the exact assignment can still evolve. At this stage the board makes clear that only a selected subset of the inventory is intended for use, rather than the full stock.
